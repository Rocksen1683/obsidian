The *short-term scheduler*  deals with which active/available process should be executed next. It is the most *frequently* used scheduler in comparison to the [[Long-Term Scheduling]] and [[Medium-Term Scheduling]].

The *short-term scheduler* is triggered whenever an event occurs that may lead to the blocking of the current running process or that ,ay provide an opportunity to preempt a currently running process. Examples:
- Clock Interrupts 
- I/O Interrupts 
- OS calls 
- Signals
